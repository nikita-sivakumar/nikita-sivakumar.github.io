Nikita Sivakumar

I am a PhD candidate in Biomedical Engineering at Johns Hopkins Univeristy. Under the mentorship of Dr. Jude Phillip and Dr. Feilim Mac Gabhann, I am developing data-driven and mechanistic computational pipelines to 1) identify single-cell lymphocyte motility behaviors and 2) map these behaviors to emergent function during the immune response. In addition to my thesis work, I have had the opportunity to spearhead several projects that apply machine learning to improve precicision healthcare in collaboration with clinicians at Johns Hopkins. Prior to graduate school, I received a B.S. in Biomedical Engineering along with minors in computer science and engineering business from the University of Virginia (UVa). At UVa, I worked with Dr. Shayn Peirce-Cottler and Dr. Matthew Lazzara to create a multi-scale mechanistic model that elucidated how dynamic differential adhesion between cells induce emergent multi-cell patterning during development and disease. I am deeply passionate about integrating data-driven and mechanistic modeling approaches to gain mechanistic insight into complex biological processes.
