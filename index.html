Research Projects

Data-driven, mechanistic model elucidates how single-cell lymphocyte motility behaviors drive B-cell : T-cell interactions during the antibdoy response.

Lymphocytes rely on their ability to move to surveil immune microenvironments and engage in critical cell-cell interactions that drive the adaptive immune response. Within germinal centers, B-cells and T-cells rely on motility to engage in B-T interactions that drive antibody affinity maturation. To quantify how single-cell motility properties of lymphocytes enable their ability to interact with each other we developed PRISMM: Pipeline for Recapitualating cell-cell Interactions from Spatial Motility Modelling:

  • PRISMM first uses unsupervised machine learning to identify emergent lymphocyte motility behaviors from three-dimensional, time-lapse intravital images of mouse germinal centers.
  • PRISMM then uses mechanistic, agent-based simulations to recapitulate observed motility behaviors at single-cell resolution and predict how these behaviors influence B-T interactions.
  • Our pipeline suggests that B-cells move slower, while T-cells take on faster and more exploratory trajectories. Moreover, our simulations indicate that slow-moving B-cells can experience comparable unique cell-cell interactions with T-cells as fast-moving B-cells. This suggests that the natural decision of B-cells to move slower may enable these cells to conserve energy for other processes without sacrificing surveillance ability.

Identifying emergent signatures of cell-free DNA methylation signatures.

As our cells proliferate, DNA is naturally relesed by cells. Analyzing the methylation signatures of cell-free DNA collected by liquid biopsy can help us trace which tissues these cel-free DNA originated from. Here we analyzed cell-free DNA methylation signatures from a cohort of RUSH patients.

  • Unsupervised clustering of the signatures indicated three predominant signatures: erythrocyte-dominated, immune-dominated, and cariovascular-dominated signatures.
  • Patients with the cardiovascular cell-free DNA signature were more likely to develop cardiovascular disease.
  • Patients with cardiovascular and immune cell-free DNA signatures were more likely to develop frailty.

Machine learning to map standardly available clinical measurements to gold-standard pressure-volume loop phenotypes in pulmonary hypertension patients.

Deriving pressure volume loops is the gold standard for measuring coupling between the right ventrial and pulmonary artery (RV - PA coupling) in patients with pulmonary hypertension. While RV-PA coupling critically predicts prognosis, this is a highly invasive and expensive clinical procedure. We sought to predict how standarly available measures of the heart (e.g. hemodynamics, MRI) could be predictive of RV-PA coupling states.

  • We applied unsupervised clustering to derive three novel PV-loop phenotypes with distinct times to clinical worsening
  • We trained a supervised machine learning model that integrated standardly available measurements of the heart to predict PV-loop phenotypes with high accuracy.

Multi-scale modeling predicts how dynamically-induced differential adhesion between cells drives emergent multi-cell patterning.

Dynamic cell-cell signaling processes induce differential adhesion between cells, which can lead to emergent tissue-level patterning during development and disease. While we know that within a static system, differential adhesion behavior lead to more adhesive cell types being surrounded by less adhesive counterparts the role of cell-cell signaling in inducing these dynamic adhesion behaviors and its effect on multi-cell patterning was not well-characterized.

  • We developed an agent-based simulation of synthetic gene circuit in which initially non-adhesive cells engage in bi-directional signaling to induce the expression of distinct adhesion proteins. In our model, we can perturb cell-cell signaling parameters and differential adhesion parameters.
  • We validated that our simulations can accurately recapitulate experimentally observed multi-cell patterns.
  • We developed a novel sensitivity analysis pipeline that leverages unsupervised learning to identify emergent multi-cell patterns from our simulations. We then mapped cell-cell signaling and differential adhesion behaviors to the likelihood of forming distinct multi-cell patterns.

Teaching

I am deeply passionate about teaching! As an undergradaute and graduate student I have developed and taught 5 undergraduate courses that focus on integrating data science and mechanistic modelling to answer inter-disciplinary ressearch questions. Recent courses include:

  • Mechanistic & Data-Driven Insights into Disease. A two-week short-course focused on introducing students computational systems biology approaches. Students learn and apply agent-based modeling, signaling network modeling, and deep learning to a series of biologial questions.
  • Introduction to Data-Driven Computational Modeling. A two-credit, semester-long course focused on integrating machine learning and agent-based simulation to develop data-driven, mechanistic models. Students learn principles of ML, ABM, and their integration. They also embark in team projects where they develop their own computational model to answer a research question of their choice.
